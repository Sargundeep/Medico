ABOUT LUNG CANCER

Lung cancer is often diagnosed in the late stages of the disease, largely because there are few or no symptoms in the early stages. Having an awareness of the risks and reducing them, plus early detection, can change a personâ€™s outcome.

STAGE 1

Small Tumor, minimally invasive and has not spread at all in one lung.

STAGE 2

Tumor grows larger, starts to spread slightly in one lung.

STAGE 3

Cancer starts to spread from the lungs to the lymph nodes and to nearby organs, like the esophagus and trachea.

STAGE 4

Cancer has spread widely through the lungs and body and may affect outside organs.
